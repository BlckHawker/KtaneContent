Fill in some cells of a 6×6 grid such that it creates a pattern resulting in a word in Braille. Numbers outside the grid tell you the lengths of runs of filled in squares within that row or column.

Stage 1: Convert each displayed fruit/color combination to a Nonogram clue as shown in Table F. The cyclic sequence of fruits will start at the bottommost row, continue upwards to the top, then continue from the left column towards the right. The start and end of the sequence is not given. However, there is only one way to place the start and end of the sequence along the outside of the grid such that it results in exactly one valid Nonogram puzzle.

After completing the Nonogram, the filled in cells will result in a word written in Braille. Tap the button when the position of the first character of the serial number in table N matches the highlighted fruit at the same position in the cyclic sequence.

Stage 2: Enter the word by tapping the button when the correct sections of the alphabet and individual letters are lit.

Hold the button to return to stage 1 at any time.
